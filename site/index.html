A political model with three poles

Politics begins when real virtues collide.

The Political Prism does not ask whether you are left or right. It asks which virtue you protect first when Freedom, Equality, and Stability cannot all be maximized at once.

The three moral axes

The model is a triangle because every answer moves toward one sacrifice.

01

Equality

Standing against domination. It asks whether people meet as moral equals or as masters and dependents.

02

Freedom

Agency against absorption. It asks what the individual can own, say, choose, trade, refuse, and exit.

03

Stability

Continuity against collapse. It asks what a society can preserve, coordinate, and become across time.

How the quiz is scored

The quiz measures priorities under tradeoff, not team identity.

Questions carry vectors.

Supporting or opposing a prompt moves your point toward Equality, Freedom, or Stability.

Answers are reversible.

The quiz page keeps progress in a compact URL, so you can leave, resume, and still stay local.

Results explain instinct.

You get what you protect first, tolerate second, sacrifice most, and where your blind spot may be.

Source, privacy, and sharing

Open source. Local-only. Shareable.

Open sourceThe static code and question file are published for inspection and reuse.

Local-onlyScoring happens in your browser. There is no backend account or answer database.

ShareableQuiz progress and results are encoded into compact URLs you can copy.
